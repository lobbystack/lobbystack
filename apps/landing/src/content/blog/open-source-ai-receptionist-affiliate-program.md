## The program in plain English

LobbyStack affiliates earn 20% commission on qualifying payments from referred customers for the first 12 months after attribution.

The core terms:

| Term | LobbyStack |
| --- | --- |
| Commission | 20% |
| Duration | First 12 months after attribution |
| Holding period | 30 days |

## Why open source changes the pitch

Closed AI receptionist tools ask buyers to trust a black box with calls, appointment requests, customer details, and escalation rules. That can work for many businesses. It can also stall a deal when a buyer asks technical questions.

LobbyStack gives affiliates stronger material:

- **Code inspection.** A skeptical buyer can review the product instead of relying on sales copy.
- **Self-hosting.** Teams can run LobbyStack on their own infrastructure when they need that control.
- **Bring-your-own providers.** Self-hosted deployments can use their own Convex, Twilio, OpenAI, calendar, analytics, billing, and email accounts.
- **Extensibility.** Agencies and developers can adapt the stack for client workflows without rebuilding the receptionist layer from scratch.
- **Hosted cloud.** Nontechnical businesses can still choose the managed product and avoid infrastructure work.

That combination matters in phone automation. A chatbot can bury mistakes in a transcript. A phone system fails in front of a customer.

If you recommend LobbyStack, you can talk about the operational details buyers care about: call routing, SMS, booking, human handoff, transcripts, recordings, usage, billing, and provider control.
